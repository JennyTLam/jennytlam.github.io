HotPlate, Northwestern StartUp

Entrepreneurial Design Thinking and Communication Project

My team worked on designing and prototyping a dish-specific food review mobile application to improve HotPlate app users' dining experience by helping them discover popular cuisine and restaurants in the downtown Evanston area. Through interviews with the client HotPlate executive team, food review industry experts, analyzing user and performance testing, and food app market research, we wireframed product features and refined our project's business model canvas. Our deliverables included app prototype created using proto.io, an online wireframing platform, the final report , and team presentation .

Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

Design Thinking and Communication Project

Focused on human centered-design, this fundamental engineering course partnered with client Shirley Ryan AbilityLabtasked us with building a non-permanent handrail extension to help a child with arthropogosis, a congenital joint disorder, ascend stairs safely, comfortably, and confidently within their home. By conducting thorough user observation in the design environment, analyzing aspects of competitive and model products, and testing different iterations of mockup ideas, our team built and refined our Step-By-Step Handrail Extension prototype for presentation at Northwestern's Winter Quarter DTC Design Exposition.

Northeastern Illinois University

Underwater Robotics Program Instructor Assistant

While exploring and learning more about remotely operated vehicles, I trained high school students to use PVC cutters, soldering irons, hand drills, and computer-aided design software to build underwater SeaPearch robots as part of a Chicago After School Matters summer program at NEIU. Some of my additional administrative tasks included updating program attendance, managing inventory and equipment, and reporting to the instructor on the students' progress. Watching young minds working together, tinkering, and customizing their bots was heartwarming, and inspired me to continue pursuing creative engineering design projects as I entered college.
