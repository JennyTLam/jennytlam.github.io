Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

Design Thinking and Communication Course Project

Focused on human centered-design, this fundamental engineering course tasked us with building a non-permanent handrail extension to help a child with arthropogosis, a congenital joint disorder, ascend stairs safely, comfortably, and confidently within their home. By conducting thorough user observation in the design environment, analyzing aspects of competitive and model products, and testing different iterations of mockup ideas, our team built and refined our Step-By-Step Handrail Extension prototype for presentation at Northwestern's Winter Quarter DTC Design Exposition.

Northeastern Illinois University

Underwater Robotics Program Instructor Assistant

While exploring and learning more about remotely operated vehicles, I trained high school students to use PVC cutters, soldering irons, hand drills, and computer-aided design software to build underwater SeaPearch robots. Some of my additional administrative tasks included updating program attendance, managing inventory and equipment, and reporting to the instructor on the students' progress. Watching young minds working together, tinkering, and customizing their bots was heartwarming, and inspired me to continue pursuing creative engineering design projects as I entered college.
